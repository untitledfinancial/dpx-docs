## Regulatory Framework & Tailwinds

Untitled_ is building for compliance across three jurisdictions from day one.
This is not retroactive — it is structural.

**United States**
- Protocol positioned strictly as a transfer mechanism, not a security
- KYC/AML handled via licensed banking partner integrations
- Compliance framework built to satisfy FinCEN, OCC, and state MSB requirements
- **GENIUS Act** — Senate stablecoin bill creating federal framework,
  legitimizing programmable stablecoins as payment infrastructure
- **US Executive Order on Digital Assets (2025)** — policy-level endorsement

**United Kingdom — FCA**
- Architecture designed to comply with FCA's Payment Services Regulations (PSR)
- Stablecoin positioned as e-money instrument, not a speculative asset
- FCA's crypto asset registration framework (FSMA 2023) compliance pathway active
- UK Financial Services and Markets Act 2023 creates explicit stablecoin regime
- Banking partner model delegates regulated activity to FCA-authorised institutions

**European Union — MiCA**
- Markets in Crypto-Assets Regulation (MiCA) — live since June 2024
- DPX architecture classifies as e-money token (EMT) or asset-referenced token (ART)
  under MiCA's defined categories, not an unregulated instrument
- MiCA's explicit stablecoin regime creates the first clear EU compliance path
- ESMA and EBA guidance followed for cross-border settlement under MiCA Title III
- Banking partner model routes regulated e-money functions through MiCA-licensed entities

**Additional tailwinds**
- **Basel III/IV digital asset guidance** — banks now have regulatory clarity
  to integrate stablecoin settlement on institutional balance sheets
- **FedNow (US)** — proving instant settlement demand at scale;
  DPX is the cross-border programmable equivalent
- **BlackRock, Fidelity, JPMorgan tokenization** — institutional validation
  accelerating regulator comfort with on-chain settlement infrastructure
