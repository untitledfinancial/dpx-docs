# DPX — For Treasury Teams

> DPX is cross-border settlement infrastructure for institutional treasury operations. It plugs into any TMS via webhook or REST API, checks macro conditions before every settlement, documents ESG impact automatically for CSRD/SFDR reporting, and executes without manual approval. The rail — not the FX desk.

## The first use case: intercompany settlement

A multinational moves funds between its own subsidiaries. Both sides are controlled by the same treasury team. DPX is ideal here because:
- No counterparty trust required — both ends are yours
- Oracle conditions are checked automatically before every transfer
- ESG documentation is generated on-chain with each settlement
- The saving vs. bank wire is immediate and measurable at any volume

At $10M/month intercompany volume (Sovereign tier), DPX costs 1.130% all-in vs. 2.50–6.00% via bank wire. That is $119,000–$487,000 saved per $10M.

## How it plugs into your TMS

DPX accepts payment instructions via webhook. Any system that can POST JSON can trigger a settlement:

```
POST https://agent.untitledfinancial.com/webhook
{
  "amount": 500000,
  "currency": "USD",
  "destination_currency": "EUR",
  "recipient": "0xRecipientWalletAddress",
  "reference_id": "INV-2026-001",
  "type": "intercompany"
}
```

The Settlement Agent maps common TMS field names automatically. No custom integration code required for Kyriba, SAP TRM, or any REST-capable treasury system.

## What happens on every settlement

1. Stability Oracle checks 9 signal layers — climate, commodities, macro, FX, basket, yield curve, infrastructure, geopolitical risk, USD structural health
2. Claude reasons about whether conditions are right: STABLE = execute, CAUTION = hold if large amount, UNSTABLE = hold
3. Router executes on-chain: fees deducted, net transferred to recipient wallet, ESG fee to impact pools
4. IPFS receipt generated — permanent, auditable, accepted by CSRD/SFDR auditors

## Oracle conditions and settlement timing

The oracle tells you when to settle, not just that it settled:

| Oracle status | Score | DPX action |
|---|---|---|
| STABLE | 90–100 | Execute immediately |
| CAUTION | 75–89 | Execute under $100K — hold larger amounts |
| UNSTABLE | < 75 | Hold — notify treasury, retry when conditions improve |

CAUTION signals include: yield curve inverting, FX volatility elevated, shipping disruption, energy stress. The oracle provides 30–90 day early warning on these conditions — not reactive monitoring.

## ESG and CSRD compliance

DPX generates on-chain ESG documentation with every settlement:
- Score calculated from 6 institutional sources (World Bank, UN, IMF, OECD, SEC, Climate Monitor)
- IPFS receipt with verifiable proof — accepted by CSRD/SFDR auditors
- ESG fee (0–0.50%) redistributed 100% to verified impact pools: Ocean Conservation (30%), Renewable Energy (25%), Forest Preservation (20%), Climate Action (15%), Clean Water (10%)
- Directly addresses CSRD Article 8 (Taxonomy Regulation) disclosure requirements for treasury operations

## Supported assets

USDC (USD Coin), EURC (Euro Coin), USDT — all on Base mainnet. 1:1 stability maintained by stablecoin issuers (Circle, Tether). For cross-currency: DPX moves value intact, receiving system handles fiat conversion — exactly the SWIFT model, without SWIFT correspondent chain fees.
